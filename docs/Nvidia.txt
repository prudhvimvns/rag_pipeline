NVIDIA Corporation is an American technology company headquartered in Santa Clara, California.

NVIDIA was founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem.

The company is best known for designing Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) used in gaming, artificial intelligence, machine learning, and data centers.

NVIDIA's products include the GeForce series for gaming, RTX GPUs, DGX systems, and CUDA software platform.

CUDA is a parallel computing platform that allows developers to use GPUs for general purpose computing.

NVIDIA has become one of the leading companies in artificial intelligence due to the widespread use of its GPUs for training and deploying large language models.

The company serves industries such as gaming, automotive, robotics, healthcare, cloud computing, and scientific research.

Jensen Huang is the Chief Executive Officer (CEO) of NVIDIA.

NVIDIA's headquarters is located in Santa Clara, California, United States.
